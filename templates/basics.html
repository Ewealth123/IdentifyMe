Earlier this year, we sent you a notification email regarding a change to the ID verification process for the National Identity Number (NIN).

In one sentence:
 
“The Virtual National Identification Numbers (vNINs) will be replacing the current NIN in the verification process.”

  What is Virtual NIN (vNIN)?    
The vNIN is a tokenized version of the current NIN.

It consists of 16 alpha-numeric characters which can be used to verify your customer’s identity.

With a vNIN, there will be no need for the holder to share their real NIN with you.

You'll know the vNIN is valid because it is issued exclusively by the ID holder who already has a NIN from NIMC.

The vNIN, like the regular NIN, will also protect your business from impersonators during onboarding.

What more do you need to know about vNIN?
  • The vNIN generated with our merchant ID can only be used once within the lifecycle of the token.
  • The vNIN expires after 72 hours.
  • The vNIN bears no resemblance to the current NIN (11-digit number issued by the NIMC).
  • Your customers can only use the phone number registered with their current NIN to log into the NIMC MWS App or via USSD.
  • NIMC plans to roll this out by January 31st 2022. From that date, merchants and service providers will not be allowed to accept the current NIN as proof of identification.
We will update you on any further changes from NIMC.
